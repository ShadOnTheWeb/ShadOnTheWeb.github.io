Queen

The Queen:

The king's second-in-command is the queen. She can move and attack in a straight line in any direction (forward, backward, sideways, or diagonally) as far across the board as she pleases (until she runs into another piece) and is therefore the most powerful piece in the game.

Queen specifications:

  • Point value: 9
  • Movement/Attack range: unlimited spaces, straight line in any direction
  • Notation symbol: Q

Bio: Garry Kasparov, Russia, 1963- : Kasparov is considered by many the greatest chess player of all time. "He became the youngest world chess champion at 22, beating Soviet grandmaster Anatoly Karpov in 1985 with an impulsive, energised attacking style that instantly gave him rock-star status in the world of chess." After a decade of retirement from chess while he dedicated himself to political activism, he is returning to play in a US tournament in August 2017. theguardian.com
