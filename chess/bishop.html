Bishop

The Bishop:

The advisors to the royalty are the bishops; this is why they stand immediately next to the king and queen. Bishops, however, have a different name depending on the language you play in. For instance, in French, the bishop is "le fou," or a jester. In Spanish, it is "el alfil," an Arabic word meaning "elephant," reminiscent of one of the bishop's historical predecessor pieces in an ancient version of chess. In Germany, they call it a "Läufer," which means "runner." There are many other personifications of this piece across many other languages. But regardless of your preferred language, the Bishop moves the same way: He moves diagonally in any direction. This means he will always remain on the color he begins on; therefore, each side has both a white-square bishop and a black-square bishop (also called kingside bishop and queenside bishop).

Bishop specifications:

  • Point value: 3
  • Movement/Attack range: unlimited spaces, diagonally in any direction
  • Notation symbol: B

Bio: José Raúl Capablanca, Cuba, 1888-1942: "A Cuban chess player who was world chess champion from 1921 to 1927. A chess prodigy, he is considered by many as one of the greatest players of all time, widely renowned for his exceptional endgame skill and speed of play."wikipedia.org
